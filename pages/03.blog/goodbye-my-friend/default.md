It has been a long time since we've seen each other. The last time was at an airport where you worked a long while ago. I thought about you recently a month ago. 

===

I hope you (the reader) allow me to be a bit selfish and express my thoughts in the best way I know.

I've avoided it by burying myself in work for the last two weeks. Admittedly, I'm not the best at dealing with situations like this to be honest with you. Not my best quality, that is for sure but this week was different. I took some time off work to see you, even if it was only for a few minutes. It was the first time I've seen you since the airport 10+ years ago. I'm ashamed to say it's been that long. You didn't look the same anymore. As it is, you've lived 10 more years since we have seen each other and that time can change people.

Then people started sharing.

The stories people have shared in the last few days talked about a person who's life connected people to other people. A genuine person who radiated such energy that it was hard not to be friends with him. 

It was also evident from the number of text messages and phone calls that contacted me about your passing. Different people from different times of my life: A close friend who now lives far away, my sister-in-law, an old church group friend, an old band mate, and even a co-worker. 

You always had a friends around, and the girls all wanted to be around you. To be honest in the beginning, I didn't really think we had a lot in common. I don't really think it mattered to you. At it's surface my story is not that special; this is what you did. You befrend, you connect, and you lift people up. This is what you did for me. Through you, I became connected with more. I saw and learned what it meant to be friendly, honest and thoughtful.

I remember we would have long chats while ~~we~~ you played guitar and I watched. Your deep thoughts that came out as wisdom and your mature outlook were things that many admired about you. However, for me it was just simply your friendship. Maybe it was the fact that you were older, maybe it was the amount of people who hung on your words, or maybe it was just you. 

I'll miss you man.
