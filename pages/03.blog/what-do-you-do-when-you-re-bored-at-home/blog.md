I've been couped up around the house for the past few days with a nasty cough. I don't feel horrible (thank you for asking) but I'd rather not subject my co-workers to coughing fits. So what do I do? Attempt to learn and how to work with hardware prototyping (i.e. the [Arduino](http://www.arduino.cc))

===

## So a bit of background

I don't consider myself a hardware guy. Software engineering has always been my bag. Hardware always intimidated me with it's resistors, transistors and capacitors that could kill you if you touch it incorrectly. Software just seemed simpler.

## Fast-forward 20 years into my career

Let's face it. Unless you're looking at building a weather station, a soil hydration detector or blink a few LEDs there isn't really any good (super) basic Arduino projects. Especially when you're trying to ease your way into the whole soldering bit. So I couldn't really see past what I couldn't do. The learning curve was pretty hard.

A few nights ago I was looking through YouTube and saw a few videos relating to USB devices that mimic keyboards and mice. There were InfoSec devices like the [RubberDucky] and the [DashBunny] while others were more focused to be local password managers. For me the use-case was templates, portable messages that are regularly typed.
