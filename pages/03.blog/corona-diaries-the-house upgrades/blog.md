It was the week of March 11. That was when a large chunk of the population (including me) started working (and schooling) from home. In the last 4 weeks, I've had to shift large parts of the house' technology to accomodate. Just a bit of context.

* Generally, the majority of our family's internet use was before and after work.
* I had some servers that we're fairly active during the day and at night.
* We have a lot of stuff on our network between Phones, tablets, Apple watches, Amazon Echos, Roku's and actual computers. (20+ devices)
* All wiring goes into my actual office room where I've bastardized the wiring.
* We don't have cable. We use a combination of Netflix, Amazon Prime, Disney+, Apple TV and Plex.
* There is also an over the air antenna for over the air TV, but that is its own blog post.

## What went haywire. Why we're making changes.

The first 3 weeks issues didn't show up immediately. As more meetings started via Skype, VPN and Zoom the issues started coming in. 
We have 20+ devices that were laying around the house (all connected to the network). I would prefer hardwired LAN cable vs wifi. This meant that I had network switches in certain areas where there were multiple devices. For example, in the family room we have a shared iMac, a Roku, plus a network switch to allow for both to be "plugged-in". This also required an extension cord because all 3 required power. Lots of cables lying around, not wife approved but (bless her) she tolerated it.

Like most people though, the first few weeks were very reactive.

We had to get the kids some chromebooks. I setup basecamp in my office (which I am lucky to have). Luckily, I already had a stand up desk that I used to set my 9 - 5 work computer on in addition to a sitting desk. Somewhere in here, there was a decision to repurpose a couple of computers.

_**I was resetting the cable modem, the main switch, and router on a daily basis.**_ I even considered putting in plug-in timers (the kind you use for Christmas lights) to trigger these resets; I didn't do that. I opted to buy more stuff and "redo" my network topology.

## A little bit of essentialism.

So before I even did any upgrades, I went through and turned off / removed devices that were plugged into the network that weren't being used. There were a few devices that fell into this category.

* An unused Galaxy Tab A tablet
* A Vera controller that never got unplugged.
* A couple of Amazon smart plugs
* A few unused Rokus (ones that get repurposed when upgrades happen)

## Home network and upgrades.

My old router was collapsing under the network traffic. At this time, I also thought that there was something going on with my network switch. The whole network was collapsing and various things needed to be unplugged at least once a day. Before I get into the details, I'd like to say _I am not a network engineer and I also don't remember the original rationale for my decisions._

Like most canadians, I use one of the big three providers in Canada. I have the cable modem, a router and a centralized switch where all the network plugs throughout the house connects to. The cable modem has a passtrough that connects to the router, and I was only using 1 port in the router to connect that main switch throughout the house. To make matters worse, I also had 3 other statelite network switches to further split things out in the various network runs. This apparently resulted in network collissions.
